About Us

The organization hereby constituted will be called HOL (Hearts of Lavender) NPO and shall be known as HOL going forward.
The NPO was founded on 10th of January 2022.
Its shortened name will be HOL (Hereinafter referred to as the organization).


MISSION

Reduce and prevent the ever-growing rate of teenage pregnancy, and combat the prevalence of sexually transmitted diseases and infections.


OUR BELIEF

To empower and promote the vulnerable and disadvantaged youth members of the society towards
self reliance and sustainable development through capacity strengthening, education and research.


VISION

Educated and empowered youth that lives their lives
fully with hopes and equal opportunities



Objectives


Educate

Providing honest and adequate sexual and sexuality education to the youth of South Africa.
The sexual and sexuality education will be done in the form of seminars that will be provided to school children weekly for four weeks. The aim of these seminars is to teach children what they need to know regarding sexual intercourse and sexual identity. As an organization we understand that prevention (in the form abstinence) is better than a cure but, in the instance, where prevention is no longer the option, we aim to provide the materials and information which will assist in reducing the rate of teenage pregnancy and sexual transmitted diseases.

Safe Space Creation

To create safe spaces (e.g shelters or group homes) where victims of assault, abuse and children of neglect can go to if their home environment is not safe to live in. These safe spaces will assist the community that they are in by:
Acting as halfway houses that provide educational and recreational activities for children who come from violent and neglectful homes.
Providing extra knowledge and tutorage. These safe spaces will be open mainly to children but will also be made available to victims (families or individuals) of abuse (sexual or physical).

Create A Sustainable Network Of Support Individuals

This network will consist of individuals who work in these various fields: social workers, police officers, teachers, lawyers, psychiatrists, psychologists as well as other community members and leaders.
This network of individuals will assist the community they are in with:
Providing support to victims of domestic and inter-partner abuse as well as any and all types of sexual assault. The support will come in efforts involving reporting crimes to the relevant authorities, as well as assisting them with coping with the crimes perpetrated against them, be it in the form of providing counselling or therapy, obtaining restraining orders, shelters and whatever else they may need.
